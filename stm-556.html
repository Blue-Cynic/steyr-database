Development History

By the late 2000s, it was apparent to Steyr that some people are not fans of the bullpup configuration. Common complaints included: the manual of arms was complicated, the triggers feel terrible, they are often not ambidextrous, and are often more complex to maintain. Given the rise of Heckler and Koch's HK416 and the request of countries for more conventional rifle designs, Steyr decided to work on a new rifle that was essentially an AUG in functionality.

They settled with the AR style or pattern in designing the new weapon given its ubiquity amongst Western nations which made it an interesting proposition for there would be very little problems for an individual trained on AR style weapons to use this new rifle over a bullpup like the AUG.

By 2012, the weapon was finished and was not exactly given attention by the gun industry nor governments. It was only in 2017 when Steyr had a chance for this weapon, when the Bundeswehr decided it wanted to replace their aging G36s with a new weapon. They collaborated with German defense company Rheinmetall, which was known for their armored vehicles, in order to setup a means to produce the weapon in Germany.

Despite the gun possessing the reliability of the AUG, its quick detach barrel system, and the ease of use of the AR, the weapon would lose to the HK416. Some speculated it lost due to politics for Heckler and Koch has the Bundeswehr by its neck. Regardless of the reasons, it seems this one weapon is doomed to fade away as a forgotten weapon.

Notable Features

Feature 1

The weapon is an AR style AUG in every sense of the word. This means individuals trained or used to the AR pattern has an easy time adjusting to this weapon.

Feature 2

Possesses the quick barrel detach system of the AUG. Making it a mission configurable weapon like its bullpup parent.

Feature 3

The weapon possesses ambidextrous controls. It is then able to be comfortably used by left handed shooters because it possesses a magazine and bolt release on both sides of the gun, which is a one-up against most AR-15s for they only have right handed controls.
