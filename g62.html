Development History

The weapon or more properly, the upgrade kit for the G36 began in 2018. Steyr heavily used feedback from Spanish troops for they too use the G36. Steyr would collaborate with Wilcox, an American company known for weapon attachments in order to make the weapon much more modular than the G36.

The G62 was designed by Steyr in an attempt to answer the many shortcomings of the G36 for it was a stopgap rifle of the Bundeswehr. Complaints of the G36 include: it is said to melt under heavy fire for its polymer body cannot stand the heat, it loses zero overtime, and that it uses its own magazines and not the AR-15 magazine which is NATO standard.

Steyr worked hard to combat and solve the many issues of the G36, and it seems troops like the upgrades. Steyr hopes that countries that widely use the G36 would settle with ordering the G62 upgrade kit as a cheap means of upgrading the weapons of their troops for replacing their guns would be costlier and take more time thanks to the procurement stage, the retraining of troops for a new gun, and the ordering of parts to keep the guns maintained by armorers.

Notable Features

Metal Receiver

Made of metal unlike the standard G36 whose body is made of polymer. This polymer body led to complaints of the gun melting after heavy use.

Improved Barrel

Has a superior barrel over the G36 which can handle more sustained fire and maintain accuracy.

Magazine Compatibility

Unlike the G36, the G62 can use the G36's proprietary magazines or AR-15 magazines.
