The History of Steyr Arms

160+ Years of Austrian Engineering Excellence

Since 1864, Steyr Arms has been hard at work in firearms innovation, designing and producing some of the world's most respected military and sporting weapons. From the industrial heart of Austria, Steyr has built a legacy of precision engineering, innovative design, and unwavering quality that spans three centuries.

The Founding Era (1864-1918)

1864 - 1918

Birth of an Industrial Giant

On April 16th, 1864, Josef Werndl founded the “Josef & Franz Werndl & Company, weapon factory and sawmill in Oberletten” with his brother Franz. This would be the foundation for the company.

In 1867, the Werndl-Holub breech-loading rifle became the standard rifle of the Austrian Army, being the company's first major success.

By 1869, the company was converted to a stock corporation named Österreichische Waffenfabriksgesellschaft (ŒWG) to handle increasing orders.

In the 1880s, Steyr introduced the Mannlicher straight-pull repeating rifles, becoming one of the largest weapon producers in the world by the 1890s, exporting to over 20 countries.

The company diversified into bicycles in 1894 and automobiles in 1916, while continuing firearms production.

Key Milestones

  • 1864: Company founded by Josef Werndl
  • 1867: Werndl-Holub rifle adopted by Austrian Army
  • 1869: Converted to ŒWG stock corporation
  • 1885: Introduction of Mannlicher M.85 repeating rifle
  • 1886: First straight-pull hunting rifle M.86
  • 1891: Production exceeds 500,000 rifles annually
  • 1894: Start of bicycle production
  • 1903: Introduction of Original Mannlicher-Schönauer
  • 1912: New factory in Steyr
  • 1912: Introduction of Steyr-Hahn pistol
  • 1915: Opening of historic Weapons Hall
  • 1916: Start of car production

The Interwar Period (1918-1939)

1918 - 1939

Adaptation and Survival

After World War I, firearms production was banned in 1918, forcing them to branch out into vehicles and other products.

In 1926, the company was renamed Steyrer Werke AG and began producing off-road trucks.

Firearms production restarted in 1930 after easing restrictions, through cooperations with Swiss and German firms.

In 1934, it merged with Austro-Daimler-Puchwerke A.G. to form Steyr-Daimler-Puch A.G.

The 1938 Anschluss by Nazi Germany incorporated the company into Reichswerke Hermann Göring for military production.

Key Milestones

  • 1918: Ban on firearms production post-WWI
  • 1926: Renamed to Steyrer Werke AG
  • 1930: Restart of firearms production
  • 1934: Merger to Steyr-Daimler-Puch A.G.
  • 1938: Incorporation into Reichswerke Hermann Göring

Post-War Reconstruction (1945-1969)

1945 - 1969

Rebuilding and Innovation

After World War II ended in 1945, weapons production ceased until 1950, when Allied permission allowed resumption of Mannlicher-Schönauer sporting rifles.

In the late 1960s, Steyr introduced the SSG 69 sniper rifle, the first serially produced high-precision rifle with a compound stock, and a new hunting rifle that became a bestseller.

Key Milestones

  • 1945: Cessation of weapons production post-WWII
  • 1950: Resumption of sporting rifle production
  • 1969: Launch of SSG 69 sniper rifle
  • 1969: Introduction of new hunting rifle with compound magazine

The Modern Era (1970-Present)

1970 - Present

Innovation and Global Success

In the late 1960s, development began on the AUG assault rifle, introduced in 1977 and adopted by the Austrian Army as StG 77.

In 1989, the firearms division became independent as Steyr Mannlicher AG.

The 1990s and 2000s saw new and innovative precision rifles and pistols like the Steyr Scout (1998), M-Series pistols (1999), SSG 04 (2004), HS .50 (2004), and SSG 08 (2008).

In 2004, headquarters moved to Raming Valley.

The company was renamed Steyr Arms in 2019 to reflect international orientation.

Innovation Highlights

Bullpup Design Leadership

The Steyr AUG established the company as a pioneer in bullpup rifle design, demonstrating that compact, ambidextrous weapons could achieve exceptional reliability and accuracy.

Modular Weapon Systems

Steyr's commitment to modular design allows users to quickly configure weapons for different roles, from close-quarters combat to designated marksman applications.

Precision Engineering

The SSG series of sniper rifles represents some of the most accurate factory-produced rifles in the world, trusted by military and police snipers globally.

Material Innovation

Early adoption of synthetic materials and advanced polymers has allowed Steyr to produce lighter, more durable weapons that perform in extreme conditions.

Ergonomic Design

From the Scout rifle to the M-Series pistols, Steyr has consistently prioritized user comfort and natural handling characteristics in its designs.

Quality Standards

Steyr's commitment to Austrian manufacturing standards ensures every firearm meets rigorous quality control requirements before leaving the factory.

Legacy and Impact

Over more than 160 years, Steyr Arms has established itself as one of the world's most respected firearms manufacturers. The company's influence extends far beyond Austria, with Steyr weapons serving in militaries, law enforcement agencies, and sporting applications across six continents.

The Steyr AUG alone has been adopted by over 30 countries and has influenced countless other bullpup designs. The SSG series has set standards for precision rifle accuracy, while the company's pistols have introduced innovative features that have been widely copied by competitors.

Today, Steyr Arms continues the tradition of innovation that Josef Werndl established in 1864. The company remains committed to producing firearms that combine cutting-edge technology with traditional Austrian craftsmanship, ensuring that the Steyr name remains synonymous with quality and innovation well into the 21st century.
