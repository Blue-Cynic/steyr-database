Development History

This SMG was designed by Steyr as a conversion kit for their AUG A1. Steyr decided that it would be a nice idea if they can make their most popular weapon to date, the AUG, into an SMG. That way, countries that have already adopted their AUG will be more inclined to buy this over any other SMG.

In essence, the AUG Para is an MPi 81 that is inside of an AUG body. The Para is direct blowback, possesses a progressive trigger, has a built-in scope (thanks to the AUG A1 being integrated with one), and uses the magazies of the MPi 69. This gun essentially rendered the MPi 69 and 81 outdated the moment it rolled out in the 80s.

Part of the reason as to why it is a much more attractive choice than the other 2 SMGs is because one does not necessarily need to buy two weapons. One can buy an AUG A1 and then buy the Para parts kit rather than buying a rifle and an SMG. This makes it a cheap option if you happen to already own the AUG A1 or are planning on buying it. One simply needs to swap out the internals of an AUG and then drop the Para parts inside of its body and vice versa.

The weapon saw relative success, being used by many law enforceent agencies, which was its target market.

Notable Features

Conversion Kit

It is a conversion kit, meaning one does not need to buy it if they already own the AUG.

Long Barrel

Has a long barrel for an SMG whilst being rather compact thanks to its bullpup design.

Weight

It may be heavy for an SMG in a polymer body, but the weight is comfortable enough and allows the gun to be light recoiling.
