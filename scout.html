Development History

The Steyr Scout was designed in the 1990s by Steyr and Jeff Cooper, a professional shooter and creator of the modern handgun shooting style. Jeff Cooper designed the Scout and the scout rifle as a concept for he wanted a lightweight weapon that was versatile. He envisioned the weapon as highly versatile, being good for hunting and self-defense. The weapon concept is essentially designed for the outdoors in mind. Its weight made it easy to carry around when hiking or exploring, alongside being away from home for weeks to months. Its chamberings are mostly full power rifle rounds, which are more than enough to take down people, big game, and predators found in the wilderness.

It has been produced by Steyr Mannlicher since 1997 and remains in production to the present. The weapon and other scout rifles that follow it are marketed towards outdoorsmen and are popular among them.

Notable Features

Integral Bipod

Features an integral folding bipod for stability.

Spare Magazine Storage

Second spare magazine stored in the butt of the rifle.

Two-Stage Magazine

The weapon has 2 locks for magazines. The first lock enables one to load loose rounds on top of the magazine, like with a stripper clip fed bolt-action rifle. The second stage allows the magazine to actually feed ammunition to the gun.
