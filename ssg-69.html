Development History

The SSG 69 was designed to replace the aging SSG 98k, which were Kar 98ks that have been modified to be sniper rifles. As Steyr states, the SSG 69 was the first ever sniper rifle to have a synthetic stock instead of wood and to use cold hammer-forged barrels. The weapon for its time was incredibly advanced that it would become the standard for modern sniper rifles.

This is arguably Steyr's most popular sniper rifle that they offer, being used by many nations in their militaries and law enforcement agencies. It has seen many conflicts across the globe. It is a precise weapon, especially for its time as many competitions were won with this rifle. This alongside the Accuracy International Arctic Warfare rifles would set the precedent for today's modern precision rifles.

  • SSG 69 PII: Additional calibers.
  • SSG 69 PIV: Short barrel for subsonic ammo.

Notable Features

Synthetic Stock

Fiberglass-reinforced polymer stock. First of its kind, makes it rather lightweight for its time.

Two-Stage Trigger

Allows one to set the trigger into varying pressures. First stage sets up the second stage, The second stage is what fires the weapon and requires very little pressure to engage it. The first stage essentially acts as a buffer to prevent the gun from going off from minor vibrations alone. The second stage is sensitive in order to prevent the gun from moving as much as possible once the trigger is pulled, perfect for precision shooting.

Cold Hammer-Forged Barrel

For durability and accuracy, one of the first of its kind. Increases barrel lifespan.
