Development History

The HS .50 is an anti-materiel rifle that Stey offers. The weapon was designed by Heinrich Fortmeier. The first version had no magazine and had to be loaded with a new round every shot.

Steyr would come out with the .460 Steyr cartridge for the gun. The reason in doing so is because certain states in the USA have banned .50 caliber cartridges, and that includes California. The .460 Steyr was designed as loophole from these regulations and possesses similar power to the .50 BMG.

The weapon is commonly mistaken for a Middle Eastern gun and is often associated with terrorists as a result. The reason as Steyr states, is that a country in the Middle East placed an order of HS .50s and likely, the guns would end up with militants who then decided to make clones of it. Iranians, Syrians, and Palestinians make unlicensed copies of the weapon. These clones are often found in many conflicts in the Middle East.
