Development History

The DMR 762 is an AR style precision rifle that Steyr designed alongside EKO Cobra, Austria's counterterrorism unit. The weapon uses a short stroke gas piston, which is becoming the common operating system for self-loading rifles in the Western world.

Unlike standard AR style rifles which have a top mounted charging handle, the DMR 762 has a charging handle to its side instead. The reason for this is it makes operating the charging handle a lot easier when in the prone position, something expected for marksmen. Much like the STM-556, the weapon possesses ambidextrous controls so left handed and right handed shooters can comfortably use them. The gun is able to be folded, which is very rare for many AR style rifles because they possess a buffer tube to hold the buffer spring of the gun's mechanism behind the gun which prevents foldable stocks from being used. This makes storing the weapon a lot easier.
